# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This README is the central entry point for all project management process documentation at OctoAcme. Use it to quickly locate, reference, or review any phase of our delivery approach — whether you're onboarding or looking to refresh your knowledge of a specific process.

---

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a structured, end-to-end project management lifecycle to deliver work consistently and with high quality. Each phase of the lifecycle is backed by a dedicated process document that defines goals, key steps, roles, and artifacts. The sections below summarize each phase and link to the full documentation.

---

## Project Lifecycle Summary

### 1. Initiation
Validate the need, align stakeholders, and define project goals. Teams use a lightweight one-pager template to capture the problem statement, proposed solution, success criteria, and initial scope. Stakeholder sign-off marks the transition to planning.

→ [Project Initiation Guide](octoacme-project-initiation.md)

---

### 2. Planning
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable backlog. This phase covers work breakdown, acceptance criteria, dependency mapping, effort estimation, and timeline definition. The output is a fully groomed backlog ready for execution.

→ [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md)

---

### 3. Execution & Tracking
Coordinate day-to-day delivery using pull requests and a shared project board. Teams track task status, surface blockers, and report progress through regular stand-ups and milestone reviews. The project board is the single source of truth for work in flight.

→ [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

---

### 4. Risk Management & Communication
Identify, assess, and mitigate risks using a risk register. Teams assign owners to each risk, define mitigation strategies, and maintain a regular communication cadence with stakeholders. Escalation paths are clearly defined to avoid surprises.

→ [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

---

### 5. Release & Deployment
Standardize feature releases to reduce risk and improve observability. This phase covers pre-release quality gates, deployment checklists, scheduling, rollback procedures, and capturing release notes for stakeholders and future reference.

→ [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)

---

### 6. Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
After major milestones, teams hold retrospectives to capture what went well, what didn't, and what to do differently. Action items are tracked and fed back into future cycles to drive continuous improvement across the organization.

→ [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md)

---

### 7. Roles & Personas
Defined personas clarify each team member's responsibilities and communication expectations throughout the project lifecycle. Understanding who does what reduces ambiguity and keeps delivery moving.

→ [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
